Franklin Park Conservatory

Open to the public in 1884, this grand Victorian-style glass greenhouse hosts over 400 species of plants from all over the world. Various exhibits such as glass artwork and light shows are also available.

Goodale Park

Thirty-two acres of greenery, trails, tennis/basketball courts, and floral displays. Contains over 169 different species of trees, alongside with its many different cultivars.

Tom W. Davis Clocktower

A 20x40 foot clocktower at OSU. Completed in 2017, the clocktower displays not only time, but quotes from William McKinley and Roberto Clemente near the bottom. This clocktower was funded from Tom W. Davis's 1.4 million donation to the University.

Poindexter Village

One of the first public housing projects in America. Operated by James Preston Poindexter, an abolitionist and helper in the Underground Railroad. He was the first black man nominated in Ohio to run for a seat in the House of Representatives.

The Oval

Originally used as a pathway between the University Hall and library, now it's surrounded by academic building interconnected by various stone paths. Used for leisure activities, university events, and many more activities.

Field of Corn

One hundred and nine concrete corn statues. Each corn weighs 1500 pounds. Each statue is exactly the same, but the corn's are rotated in several different directions to give the illusion of each ear being completely unique. Wow

John Glenn Airport

Originally named "Port Columbus International Airport", it was unanimously approved to be changed in honor of John Glenn, the first American to orbit Earth.

Asian Festival

Giant festival drawing over 50k visitors annually. From visual art shows to educational exhibitions, the festival educates and entertains guests about cultures and traditions of the people from all Asian countries!

Columbus Zoo

Founded in 1927, this Zoo and Aquarium holds more than 7000 animals, with over 800 of them being from a unique species. The Zoo contains animals from all regions of the globe, and it's live coral exhibit being one of the largest in the United States.

COSI

First opened on March 29, 1964, this science museum has the largest outreach education program of any science museum in America. COSI contains 7 main exhibition areas, each hosting many different interactive activities and gallerys.

Arnold Statue

An 8 feet tall bronze statue of Arnold Schwarzenegger. Columbus hosts the annual Arnold Sports Festival where people gather to win in a contest of BodyBuilding and Fitness.

Columbus City Council for a Celebration of Black History

An event held by the Columbus City Council in order to present a celebration of African American History in the success of shaping Columbus. During the event, the council will honor many recipients with the James Preston Poindexter Award.

Dragon Boat Race

The year was 2010 when the Asian Festival promoted an exhibition of dragon boat racing on the Scioto Mile river in downtown Columbus, Ohio. The non-profit, arts-and-culture organization recruited two paddling teams and choreographed the event. Thus was born the city’s first Dragon Boat Race. Open to the public, and free of charge, these celebrations fall within the month of May, highlighting the Asian Pacific American Heritage Month and bringing greater visibility to the Asian-American communities in Central Ohio.

Columbus Pride Festival and Parade

The first Pride march in Columbus, Ohio was held in June 1981. Only 200 people were in attendance, and some even wore bags over their heads to conceal their identities. In 2019, we were joined by over 13,000 parade marchers, representing a vast array of non-profits, community organizations, corporate sponsors, small businesses, political candidates and activists! An estimated 850,000 people attended Pride.

Jeni's Splendid Ice Creams

Jeni Britton Bauer is an American ice cream maker and entrepreneur. A pioneer of the artisan ice cream movement, she introduced a modern, ingredient-driven style of ice cream making that has been widely emulated across the world but never duplicated. Jeni opened her first ice cream shop, Scream, in 1996, then founded Jeni’s Splendid Ice Creams in 2002.
